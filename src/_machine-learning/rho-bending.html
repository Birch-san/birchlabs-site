When few-step sampling, you may find that your image is moreorless good, but has some errors in fine detail or composition.

You can change the rho parameter of a Karras schedule to bend the denoising schedule to allocate more sigmas to the highly-noised or lightly-noised part of the schedule.

rho is typically 7, but decreasing it (e.g. to 5) will sample more from high sigmas (>2), expending more effort on high-level composition.
Conversely: increasing it (e.g. to 10) will sample more from low sigmas (<1), expending more effort on fine detail.
